What Inspired Us

A small story that inspired us to focus on this topic

Group Members

Yiwen Chen, Jia Du, Dinghong Li & Qifan Zhang

Inspiration

One of our team members, Dinghong Li, told us that his mother, who now works in a college, uses data collected from a building, such as electricity consumption, light usage, gas consumption, domestic water flowing speed and so on, to judge students' condition manually. What is more, accuracy and liability of this method of judgement has been proved by an investigation and concluded in an essay, which inspired us that it is feasible to give a vague evaluation of a building's condition in terms of keep people inside healthy and even give personal health evaluation and health suggestion according to this.

General Description

What is our project

After discussion and investigation, we want to judge 'health' from both building side and individual side.

For building

It is clear that data is directly collected from every corner of the whole building (campus, floor, room or other sector unit). In order to meet our goal from inspiration as well, firstly we work out a 'health condition value' for a location. Of course, buildings themselves will not be healthy or sick, but its condition will do influence individuals' health at a very large extent . That is why the 'health condition value' for a location matters.

This kind of monitoring is mainly designed for where sector monitoring is required, such as campuses, office buildings, houses and so on.

Compared with building monitoring, this is individual-oriented. Individuals' health will be analyzed anchored in building monitoring data. For Sector Monitoring

In this project, we use multiclass classification machine learning algorithm as our main algorithm. We use the data of electricity, water, and gas usage, which is all sampled once per minute. Thus,the number of data per day is 4320. Then we multiply the data matrix with a 4321*10 coefficient matrix (trained previously) and choose the entry with the largest value as the health value. For Individuals

Individual health analysis is based on building ones. Similar with sector monitoring, there are still two matrices. This time, data in two matrices are combined data from sectors he or she has passed in one day. Also, the trained coefficient matrix will add time proportions. Finally, we work out a health value for the individual.

Fog Computing Inside

We mainly use Distributed Computation, one of the most important concepts used in fog computing. Instead of pushing all raw data to the cloud, all the data in one sector will be calculated and analyzed in the every fog node. For individuals, data will be directly sent to remote terminals and they will do final calculations and display it. Compared with 'cloud based model', it has following advantages:

1. Reliability by Redudancy. If one node is down, there is no destructive consequence temporarily. The whole system still works properly.

2. Privacy. In the traditional cloud system, all the raw data finally goes to the cloud, which will call great leak once poorly stored. In this fog based system, unless requested, all the raw data will not be sent to the cloud. With this kind of 'point to point' transmission, security will be much easier to achieve with the aid of cryptology.

3. Computing and Storage Saving On the Cloud. A large part of calculation and storage work distributed to fog nodes, computing and storage burden on the cloud will be greatly reduced. So will network transmission.

Challenges

1. Not enough training for the coefficient matrix. We need more data and more systematic training to get a more accurate coefficient matrix.

2. Privacy. Though solved partly by fog nodes' direct transmission, privacy is still a hard thing to judge in reality.

3. Sensor deployment. Especiall for existing building.
